Unraveling The Spiral

Introduction

The Term ‘Spiraling’ has many interchangeable names such as ‘Catastrophic Thinking,’ ‘The Upward/Downward Spiral,’ and ‘The Anxiety Spiral.’ As well as the many forms this term can take on, it can generate even more varying definitions. To deepen our understanding of ourselves and our experiences of comfort or lack of comfort while spiraling, this website intends to expand upon the orginial survey titled, ‘Subject:Spiraling,’ conducted on September 09/25/2021 with an increased number of inquiries.

Survey Questions:

  1. How do you define spiraling?
  2. What would say initiates spiraling for you frequently? (e.g. work stress, dirty dishes, looking in the mirror)
  3. How do you feel while spiraling and/or undergoing trauma/distress?
  4. Describe an experience where you felt comforted during spiraling and/or undergoing trauma/distress? (examples can be as simple as a friend calling you on the phone once every month, watching a movie or favorite show with a supporter every week, or someone purchasing groceries for you)
  5. What are some things that comfort you during spiraling?
  6. Do you feel alone while you are spiraling and/or undergoing trauma/distress?
  7. Do you feel anxious or hesitant to reach out to others while you are spiraling and why?
  8. What are some ways you feel that you can unravel your spiraling?
